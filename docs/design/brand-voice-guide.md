# Brand Voice Guide: Motson

## The Voice: The Droll Uncle

Factual and even — it just tells you the score — with the dry, avuncular warmth of the man himself: a knowledgeable aside delivered in the same breath as the facts, never a performance.

## Tone of Voice Dimensions

Positioned on the four dimensions of tone of voice (Nielsen Norman Group). Each row is its own spectrum: the score is read against **that row's poles** — **1 = the left-hand label, 5 = the right-hand label, 3 = balanced**. So on "Funny ↔ Serious", 1 is most funny and 5 is most serious.

> 1 = strongly the left-hand label · 2 = leans left · 3 = balanced · 4 = leans right · 5 = strongly the right-hand label

The **Position** column names the chosen pole in plain words and is the source of truth; the number is a convenience that must agree with it. A near-pole score is fine — many brands genuinely sit at an extreme (e.g. strongly respectful), so don't nudge a dimension toward the middle just to look balanced.

| Dimension | Score (1–5) | Position | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Funny ↔ Serious | 4 | leans serious | Mostly straight — a fan glancing for a score is not here for jokes. But a single dry, droll aside is permitted and is where the warmth lives; not a 5, because pure seriousness would lose Motson entirely. "Factual but not boring." |
| Formal ↔ Casual | 3 | balanced | Speaks plainly to a fan and uses contractions, but is never slangy, matey or exclamatory. Conversational, not casual — and never stiff enough to read as the corporate or the spreadsheet. |
| Respectful ↔ Irreverent | 2 | leans respectful | Respects the football and, above all, the reader. The only irreverence allowed is a gentle dryness about the *subject* (the fixture list, the day off), never at the user's expense. "Avuncular but not patronising." |
| Enthusiastic ↔ Matter-of-fact | 4 | leans matter-of-fact | Just tells you the score; warmth carries the feeling, not exclamation marks or hype. Not a 5, because the voice is "unbiased but not indifferent" — it cares, it simply doesn't shout. |

## Brand Voice Chart

The voice broken into 3-5 distinct personality traits. Each Do/Don't must be concrete and actionable — name the specific behaviour, and where useful the reason — not abstract guidance.

| Trait | Description | Do's | Don'ts |
|---|---|---|---|
| Avuncular | The trusted uncle reading the scores: warm, settled, on your side, never talking down. | Address the reader as a knowledgeable equal; use plain contractions ("nothing on today", "we're back tomorrow"); let warmth come through cadence and word choice. | Never explain the obvious or hand-hold ("Simply tap here!") — it patronises. No "I'm afraid" hand-wringing, no over-familiar matey-ness ("grab a brew, pal"). |
| Factual & clear | The score, the time, the status — stated cleanly and trusted to be current. | Lead with the fact (result, kick-off time, match status); use the precise football term ("Full time", "Postponed", not "Game over"); keep sentences short. | Never bury the fact under decoration or padding; never let copy read as dry, lifeless data — clarity is not the same as a spreadsheet, which is the absolute no. |
| Unbiased | Neutral between every team and nation — but caring about the game, not cold about it. | Treat all teams and results evenhandedly; describe what happened, not who "should" have won; keep tone even whether it's a thrashing or a goalless draw. | Never editorialise, take sides, or hype a "big" team; but never tip into flat indifference either — the voice cares about football, it just doesn't pick favourites. |
| Droll | A dry, deadpan aside in the same even tone as the facts — the warmth you catch a beat later. | Reserve humour for low-stakes margins (empty states, the 404, a day with no fixtures); keep it understated and singular — one quiet line, then back to business. | Never be witty, jokey, punny or quirky for its own sake; never let a quip delay or obscure a score or status the reader came for. |
| Quietly assured | Confidence expressed through restraint — the visual and verbal poise that turns dry data into something elegant. | Say less; trust whitespace, hierarchy and the floodlit palette to carry authority; let the amber score and a single deliberate touch (the mascot) do the talking. | Never crowd the page to seem busy or important; no shouty TV-graphics maximalism, gradient SaaS chrome, ad clutter, or a wall of identical cells — and never a spreadsheet. |

## Tone Shifts by Context

*Optional but recommended. Voice stays constant; tone flexes. For each context, express the change as a delta against the default scores above, in the form `Dimension: default → shifted`, followed by a short instruction. Cover high-stakes or emotional moments especially.*

| Context | Shift |
|---|---|
| Errors / stale or unavailable data | Funny ↔ Serious: 4 → 5. Drop the droll entirely; be plain, calm and honest about what's missing and when it'll be back. Never joke when the reader can't get what they came for. |
| Empty states & the 404 | Respectful ↔ Irreverent: 2 → 3. This is the one place the droll aside is most welcome — a single dry, warm line is what turns a dead end into Motson. |
| Live match in progress | Enthusiastic ↔ Matter-of-fact: 4 → 3. A touch warmer and more present to mark that it's happening now — still no hype, exclamation marks or partisanship. |

## Sample Copy

Each sample written in the chosen voice, at its default tone.

**Error message:**
> Scores are a few minutes behind — we couldn't reach the source just now. We'll catch up at the next sync; nothing's lost.

**Marketing line:**
> Every fixture, score and group table for the 2026 World Cup. Calm, current, and on your phone in a glance.

**Support reply:**
> Subscribed the calendar and the matches aren't showing? Give your calendar app a few minutes to refresh — Motson updates the feed every ten. If they're still missing after that, the feed link may have been copied short; here's the full one.

**Onboarding email opener:**
> You're all set — the World Cup is now in your calendar. Every kick-off will appear with the latest score, refreshed through the tournament. Nothing else to do; just turn up.
